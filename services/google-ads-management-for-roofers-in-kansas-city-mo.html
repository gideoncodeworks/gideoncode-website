The Kansas City Roofers Problem

Kansas City straddles the Missouri-Kansas state line, which creates a unique advertising challenge for roofing companies that serve both sides of the metro across Johnson, Wyandotte, Jackson, and Clay counties. The region's severe weather corridor delivers multiple hail events per year, with the spring storm season from April through June generating the bulk of insurance restoration work that can make or break a roofing company's annual revenue. Kansas City's diverse housing stock, from the historic brick homes of Brookside and Waldo to the newer developments in Olathe, Lee's Summit, and Liberty, means different neighborhoods have different roofing needs and search behaviors that must be targeted accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

We build separate campaigns for each side of the metro with distinct geographic targeting, budgets, and keyword strategies. This is critical because a homeowner in Overland Park, Kansas has different insurance carriers, building codes, and even weather patterns than someone in Independence, Missouri, and your ads need to reflect that. Separate campaigns also let you control budget allocation based on where your most profitable jobs come from, rather than letting Google distribute your spend evenly across both states. We use zip code-level targeting to ensure ads only show in the communities where your crews actually work.

Storm chasers typically run broad, aggressive campaigns that waste money on low-intent clicks because they are optimizing for volume rather than quality. We take the opposite approach, building tightly targeted campaigns around high-intent keywords and specific neighborhoods that just experienced storm damage. We also pre-build storm response campaigns that can be activated within hours of a major weather event, giving you a head start over companies scrambling to launch ads after the fact. Your advantage as a local Kansas City company is trust, and we build that into your ad copy, landing pages, and review strategy so homeowners choose the contractor who will still be here next year.

Outside of storm season, most Kansas City roofing companies see a cost per lead between $75-150 for repair and replacement inquiries through well-managed Google Ads campaigns. During the spring storm season, cost per click rises significantly due to competition, but so does the volume and value of leads, with average cost per lead running $100-200 for storm restoration leads that can result in $8,000-15,000 jobs. We focus on reducing wasted spend so that your effective cost per signed contract stays profitable regardless of seasonal fluctuations. Our goal is to maintain a minimum 5x return on ad spend, meaning every dollar you invest in ads generates at least five dollars in revenue.

We recommend maintaining a baseline campaign year-round with the ability to scale up rapidly during storm events. Even outside of peak storm season, Kansas City homeowners search for roof inspections before winter, ice dam prevention, gutter installation, and general roof replacement throughout the year. Pausing your campaigns entirely during slower months means you lose all the optimization data and quality score Google has built up, making it more expensive to restart when you need leads most. We adjust budgets seasonally, typically running at 40-50% of peak spending during winter months and scaling to full capacity from March through August.
