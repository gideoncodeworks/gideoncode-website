2. ACCOUNT EXECUTIVE ROLE

Position Overview

Account Executives (AEs) serve as the primary client-facing representatives for Gideon Codeworks. Core responsibilities include:

  1. Lead Generation ‚Äî Identify and qualify prospective clients
  2. Relationship Development ‚Äî Establish and nurture business relationships
  3. Needs Assessment ‚Äî Evaluate client requirements and business objectives
  4. Solution Presentation ‚Äî Align company services with client needs
  5. Deal Closure ‚Äî Secure signed agreements and initial payments
  6. Client Transition ‚Äî Transfer closed accounts to production team

Scope Limitations

Account Executives do NOT:

  • Perform website development or technical work
  • Provide post-sale technical support
  • Process payments (all transactions occur through company systems)
  • Authorize custom pricing without management approval

Contractor Status

Account Executives operate as Independent Contractors, not employees. This classification entails:

  • Flexible scheduling and work location
  • Responsibility for self-employment taxes
  • Permission to maintain other business relationships (excluding direct competitors)
  • No entitlement to employee benefits (health insurance, PTO, retirement, etc.)
  • Performance-based compensation

Performance Benchmarks

While no mandatory quotas exist, successful representatives typically maintain the following activity levels:

5. SALES METHODOLOGY

Phase 1: Prospecting

Lead Sources:

  • Search engine queries: "[location] + [business type]" ‚Äî identify businesses with inadequate web presence
  • Local business groups on social platforms
  • LinkedIn professional networking
  • Client referrals
  • Industry networking events
  • Business networking organizations (BNI, chambers of commerce)

Qualification Criteria:

  • No existing website
  • Outdated or poorly functioning website
  • Non-mobile-responsive design
  • Limited online presence beyond social media
  • Competitors with superior web presence

Phase 2: Initial Contact

Telephone Script:

"Good [morning/afternoon], this is [Name] with Gideon Codeworks. I specialize in helping [industry] businesses improve their online presence. I noticed [specific observation]. Do you have a moment to discuss?"

Email Template:

Subject: Inquiry Regarding [Business Name] Online Presence

[Name],

While researching [service type] providers in [location], I came across [Business Name]. I noticed [specific observation regarding their current web presence].

Gideon Codeworks specializes in professional website solutions for [industry] businesses. Would you be available for a brief call this week?

Regards,
[Your Name]
Account Executive, Gideon Codeworks

Phase 3: Discovery

Essential Questions:

  1. "Does your business currently have a website?"
  2. "What are your primary customer acquisition channels?"
  3. "What challenges do you face with your current online presence?"
  4. "Have you worked with web development companies previously?"
  5. "What would an ideal website accomplish for your business?"

Phase 4: Presentation

Present solutions aligned with identified needs. Key differentiators to emphasize:

  • AI chatbot included at no additional cost
  • Comprehensive service (client focuses on core business)
  • Month-to-month flexibility after initial term
  • Direct support from company personnel

Phase 5: Objection Management

ObjectionResponse Strategy
"Price is too high""What is the lifetime value of a new customer? If this generates one additional customer monthly, does the ROI justify the investment?"
"Need time to consider""Certainly. What specific aspects require consideration? I may be able to provide clarification."
"Can use DIY platforms""That's an option. The question becomes whether your time is best spent managing a website or operating your business."
"Know someone who can do it""Will they provide ongoing support? Will they be available if issues arise at critical times?"
"Previous negative experience""That's precisely why our service includes month-to-month terms after the initial period. We maintain accountability."

Phase 6: Closing

Trial Close: "Does this solution address your requirements?"

Assumptive Close: "Excellent. Let's proceed with setup. I'll need a few details..."

Scheduling Follow-up: "When would be an appropriate time to reconnect‚ÄîThursday or Friday?"

Phase 7: Transition to Production

Post-signature process:

  1. Log transaction in CRM system
  2. Collect client intake information
  3. Notify production team
  4. Send client welcome communication
  5. Proceed to next opportunity

7. PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS

Required Behaviors

  • Maintain honesty and transparency in all communications
  • Present professional appearance appropriate to context
  • Honor all scheduled appointments
  • Follow through on all commitments
  • Acknowledge knowledge limitations ("I'll research that and follow up")

Prohibited Behaviors

  • Making unauthorized commitments
  • Guaranteeing specific outcomes (rankings, leads, revenue figures)
  • Disparaging competitors
  • Employing high-pressure or deceptive tactics
  • Misrepresenting contractor status as employment

Authorized Claims

Permitted Statements:

  • "We build professional websites"
  • "Hosting, updates, and security are included"
  • "Ongoing support is provided"
  • "All websites include AI chatbot functionality"
  • "Month-to-month terms apply after initial contract"

Prohibited Statements:

  • Specific search ranking guarantees
  • Lead volume guarantees
  • Revenue increase projections
  • ROI guarantees
  • Features not included in standard packages

Pricing Authority

  • Standard pricing only; no unauthorized modifications
  • Discount requests require management approval
  • Never negotiate below published rates without authorization

9. ISSUE RESOLUTION

Cancellation Requests

  1. Remain professional
  2. Document reason for cancellation
  3. Escalate to management if issue is resolvable
  4. Process cancellation professionally if client confirms
  5. Note: Recurring commissions cease; no clawback on past payments

Payment Issues

SituationAction
Initial payment failureContact client per production notification
Recurring payment failureSystem retries automatically; may require follow-up
Disputed chargeEscalate to management immediately

Production Concerns

If client expresses dissatisfaction during production:

  1. Document the specific concern
  2. Acknowledge client frustration
  3. Escalate to management as appropriate
  4. Maintain client communication
  5. Maintain professional relationships with internal teams

Scope Creep

  • Minor clarifications: Handle within normal scope
  • Additional features/pages: "That would require the [higher tier] package"
  • Uncertain situations: Escalate to management

10. TEAM DEVELOPMENT

Eligibility Requirements

Recruitment privileges unlock after closing ten (10) verified deals.

Override Structure

Percentage of recruited representatives' monthly recurring revenue:

  • Level 1 (direct recruit): 5%
  • Level 2 (second generation): 3%
  • Level 3 (third generation): 2%

Example: Recruited representative sells Growth WaaS + Social ($502.50/month)

11. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What is the typical timeline for closing an initial deal?
A: Most representatives close their first deal within 2-4 weeks. Timeline varies based on activity volume and prior experience.

Q: Are there geographic restrictions on sales territory?
A: No. The company serves clients nationwide without territory limitations.

Q: How are non-standard client requests handled?
A: Contact management. Accommodation may be possible, or an explanation will be provided.

Q: Is technical expertise required?
A: No. Product knowledge is necessary; technical implementation knowledge is not.

Q: Can existing website owners be prospects?
A: Yes. Many clients are website rebuilds. Poor existing sites represent opportunities.

Q: Can I sell to friends and family?
A: Yes, though family member sales require prior management approval for commission eligibility.

Q: What happens during extended time away?
A: As an independent contractor, scheduling is flexible. Recurring commissions continue as long as clients remain active.

Q: What support exists for underperformance?
A: While no mandatory quotas exist, management provides coaching and support for representatives seeking assistance.

Q: Can personal marketing materials be used?
A: All marketing materials require management approval prior to use.

Q: How are commission balances monitored?
A: The CRM dashboard displays current and pending commission information.
